  The Goal

To destroy the enemy Queen.

  Rules

The game is played by two players on a semi-open two-dimensional field 1920 units wids and 950 units wide, littered with circular obstacles of varying radius.
Each player controls a single Queen character, who can establish different structures on those obstacles.

The field is generated randomly every game, with the obstacles being symmetrically laid out.

Queens

The Queen is the player’s only way to directly interact with the field.
The Queen is modeled as a circle with radius 20 units, and moves up to 60 units every turn.
Queen characters each start with 200 HP, and will be destroyed if this drops to zero -- if this happens, the player will lose the game.

If both Queens are destroyed in the same turn, the game will end in a draw.

Structures

A Queen character can build a structure on an obstacle that she is in contact with.
If she so chooses, a Queen can replace an existing friendly structure simply by building another one in its place.
If an enemy structure is already in place on an obstacle, a Queen cannot replace it.
If both Queens try simultaneously to build a structure on the same obstacle, neither structure will be built.

Structures can be built at no cost, and are available for use on the turn immediately following their construction.

Mining

Players will need mineral resources to win.
A Queen can establish a resource mine on an obstacle by issuing the command BUILD MINE while touching an obstacle, or using the command BUILDONOBSTACLE {id} MINE to move toward an obstacle and build a mine when touching that obstacle.

Each obstacle has a different rate at which resources can be collected, ranging from +1/turn to +5/turn.
Once a mine is established, resources will accumulate in the player’s bank over several turns.
However, the obstacle will be exhausted of resources over time (all obstacles begin the game with the same amount).

A mine will be destroyed if:

  • It runs out of resources
  • An enemy creep comes in contact with it

Creeps

In order to destroy the enemy Queen, a player will need to build creeps.
Once built, creeps follow very simple behaviours (see below), and cannot be controlled by the player.
Creeps will eventually age and die on their own, losing 1HP every turn.

There are three different creep types that can be built.
Type Cost Number Speed Range HP Training Time (turns)
MELEE 80 4 20 0 30 5
RANGED 120 2 13 200 45 8
GIANT 140 1 10 0 200 10

MELEE units are light, fast units which attack the enemy Queen, dealing 1 damage every turn they end contacting the enemy Queen.
RANGED units are slower, ranged units which move toward and attack nearby enemy creeps from a short distance.
They deal 1 damage to the their nearest enemy creep every turn they end within range of that creep. (Note: they can shoot past obstacles).
GIANT units do not attack the enemy Queen, but instead attack nearby enemy towers and break them down over time.

A player needs to build a BARRACKS with his Queen in order to create creeps.
A Queen can establish a barracks on an obstacle by issuing the command BUILD BARRACKS {type} while touching an obstacle, for example: BUILD BARRACKS RANGED, or using the command BUILDONOBSTACLE {id} BARRACKS {type} to move toward an obstacle and build a barracks when touching that obstacle.
Training creeps is explained in a section below.

Towers

With enemy creeps hunting down the Queen, a player will be well-advised to create some static defense in the form of towers.
A Queen can establish a tower on an obstacle by issuing the command BUILD TOWER while touching an obstacle, or using the command BUILDONOBSTACLE {id} TOWER to move toward an obstacle and build a tower when touching that obstacle.

The first such command will establish a small tower with a small firing radius.
A player can grow an existing tower by having the Queen stand next to it and issuing the same order again; this will increase the tower’s firing radius over several turns.
Once built, towers start to degrade slowly.
A tower will eventually degrade completely and be destroyed.
This can be delayed by regular visits by the Queen, and issuing the BUILD TOWER command again.

Every turn, towers automatically fire upon the nearest enemy creep inside their firing radius, dealing 6-8 damage.
If there are no creeps in range, but the enemy Queen is in range, a tower will fire upon it instead, dealing 1 damage.

If the enemy player is investing a lot of effort into towers, a player may wish to train GIANT creeps to knock them down.

Training Creeps

Every obstacle is numbered, starting from 0.
A player trains creeps by indicating which numbered obstacles containing a barracks he wishes to train creeps at.
Examples:

  • “TRAIN 13 6 19” - Three barracks begin training creeps
  • “TRAIN 14” - One barracks begins training creeps
  • “TRAIN” - No barracks begin training creeps.

Every barracks is built with the ability to train a single creep type.
When the train commands are sent, the player pays the total cost in resources, and indicated barracks will begin training the appropriate set of units.
After the appointed number of turns elapses, a set of creeps emerges from the barracks and begins acting by themselves on the next turn.

A barracks that is already in the middle of training cannot begin training again until the current creep set is built.
Also, such a barracks cannot be replaced by another structure.

Victory Conditions
  • Destroy the enemy Queen.
  • After 400 turns, your Queen has more HP than the enemy Queen.
Lose Conditions
  • Your Queen is destroyed.
  • Output an invalid command.

  Expert Rules

Collisions

Units cannot share space.

Draws

The game will end in a draw if after 400 turns both Queens have the same HP.

  Game Input

Initialization input
Line 1: an integer numObstacles, indicating the number of obstacles on the map.
Next numObstacles lines: 4 integers obstacleId x y radius
  • obstacleId: The numeric identifier of the obstacle
  • x y: The numeric coordinates of the obstacle's center
  • radius: The radius of the obstacle
Input for one game turn
Line 1: Three integers queenX queenY health resources
Line 2: Three integers enemyQueenX enemyQueenY enemyHealth
  • queenX queenY: The coordinates of the Queen
  • health: The current health of the Queen
  • resources: The resources available for your Queen to spend on creeps (enemy resource total not given)

Next numObstacles lines: Seven integers obstacleId resources maxMineSize structureType owner param1 param2
  • obstacleId: The numeric identifier of the obstacle
  • resources: The total number of resources remaining to be mined from this obstacle (-1 if unknown)
  • maxMineSize: The maximum rate that a mine can extract resources from this obstacle (-1 if unknown)
  • structureType:
    • -1: No structure
    • 0: Resource Mine
    • 1: Tower
    • 2: Barracks
  • owner:
    • -1: No structure
    • 0: Friendly
    • 1: Enemy
  • param1:
    • When no structure -1
    • When resource mine, the income rate ranging from 1 to 5
    • When tower, the remaining lifetime in turns before disintegrating
    • When barracks, the current progress of a set of creeps being trained or 0 if nothing is being trained
  • param2
    • When no structure -1
    • When resource mine -1
    • When tower, the attack radius measure from its center
    • When barracks, the creep type: 0 for MELEE, 1 for RANGED, 2 for GIANT

Next Line: numMyCreeps: Number of active creeps owned by you
Next numMyCreeps lines: 4 integers creepX creepY creepHealth creepType Next Line: numEnemyCreeps: Number of active creeps owned by the opponent
Next numMyCreeps lines: 4 integers creepX creepY creepHealth creepType
  • creepX creepY: Coordinates of the creep
  • creepHealth: Current HP of the creep
  • creepType: The creep type: 0 for MELEE, 1 for RANGED, 2 for GIANT
Output for one game turn
First line: One of the following
  • WAIT Do nothing
  • MOVE x y Will attempt to move towards the provided coordinates (x,y given as integers)
  • MOVETOOBSTACLE obstacleId Will attempt to move towards the obstacle indicated
  • BUILD MINE Will attempt to build a resource mine at the obstacle touched by the Queen
  • BUILD TOWER Will attempt to build or repair a tower at the obstacle touched by the Queen
  • BUILD BARRACKS type Will attempt to build a barracks at the obstacle touched by the Queen. The type must be given as either MELEE, RANGED, or GIANT.
  • BUILDONOBSTACLE obstacleId type Will attempt to build a barracks at the specified obstacle of the specified type. If not in range, the Queen will instead move towards the obstacle automatically.
Constraints
Response time for first turn ≤ 1000ms
Response time for one turn ≤ 50ms
